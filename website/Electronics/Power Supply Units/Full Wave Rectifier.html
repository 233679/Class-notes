A Full Wave Rectifier is used to convert AC to DC

Example Circuit for a Full Wave Rectifier:

Example waveform for a Full Wave Rectifier:

In a full wave rectifier, the Current follows through 2 Diodes (the other two are used to block the Current), which means the Peak Voltage (peak voltage) drops by 1.4V (Diodes have a 0.7V drop). Also, the frequency of the DC waveform is double that of the AC value. This is because the rectifier is converting both the low & high Peak Voltage of the AC waveform. However, a constant Voltage isn't output, the value is subject to Voltage Ripple.

However, the output DC waveform isn't constant, it varies by huge amounts. To help smooth out the DC Voltage a smoothing Capacitor can be added to the circuit.
